Neymar da Silva Santos Júnior

Brazilian footballer

From Santos to Stardom

Neymar da Silva Santos Júnior, commonly known as Neymar, began his football journey in São Paulo, Brazil. He joined the youth team of Santos FC at just 11 years old, where he quickly showcased his exceptional talent and flair. By 17, he made his professional debut, becoming the youngest player to score for Santos in a national tournament. His remarkable performances led the team to victory in the Copa do Brasil and the Copa Libertadores, catapulting him into the international spotlight. In 2013, he made a significant move to FC Barcelona, where he formed a legendary attacking trio with Lionel Messi and Luis Suárez, helping the team secure numerous titles, including the UEFA Champions League and multiple La Liga championships.

Global Superstar and Impact

Neymar's success continued to soar as he transitioned to Paris Saint-Germain (PSG) in 2017, where he became the world's most expensive player at that time. His dazzling dribbling skills, creativity, and goal-scoring ability solidified his status as one of the best players in the world. Beyond club football, Neymar has also made significant contributions to the Brazilian national team, playing pivotal roles in their Copa América and World Cup campaigns. Off the pitch, he has become a global icon, inspiring millions with his flair and charisma. Neymar’s journey reflects not just his talent but also his resilience and determination to overcome challenges, making him a prominent figure in the world of sports.

UEFA Top Goalscorer

Neymar and FCB steamrolled their way to a sensational treble, winning the league, cup, and UEFA titles, while scoring an impressive 39 goals.

Olympic Gold Medal

Top goal scorer

2023

Neymar is the all-time top goal scorer for Brazil's men's national soccer team, with 79 goals in 125 international appearances.
